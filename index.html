What is ZenTube?

ZenTube is an app that aggregates online videos from the libraries of YouTube, Vimeo and Vevo into programs and channels similar to cable and network TV.

ZenTube is different from other video aggregators because it crowdsources the empathy and cultural intelligence of its users to find great entertainment. ZenTube exploits the niche created by the libraries’ use of robotic video search engines and their reliance on the honesty and objectivity of the original video uploaders.

The ZenTube app will be developed for mobile and Internet TV devices, targeting in particular the smart TV device currently understood to be in development by Apple.

From a customer perspective, ZenTube offers three levels of engagement as passive viewers, active moderators and active program makers, all connect through ZenTube's social media ecosystem.

ZenTube’s business model utilises freemium app sales, premium channel subscriptions and sophisticated new media marketing, including Karma, a sponsored viral video competition channel targeted specifically at digital native Generation Z users, and celebrity-hosted special interest channels with programs designed to be shared with their followers.

ZenTube acts as an elegant frontend to the existing video collections. Current status of ZenTube includes development of the software story roadmap, user interface mock-ups and business model.

Funding is being sought for the development of a prototype app and database to achieve proof of concept for the venture.
